<span class="anchor" id="about-me"></span>

I am **Jiahe Chen**, a Ph.D. student in Computer Science and Technology at Zhejiang University. Before joining Zhejiang University in September 2025, I received my B.Eng. in Software Engineering from Zhejiang University of Technology, where I was advised by Prof. [Jianwei Zheng](https://homepage.zjut.edu.cn/zjw/).

My research interests include **trustworthy machine learning**, **computer vision**, **vision-language models**, and **AI systems**. I am particularly interested in problems where solid learning algorithms meet practical deployment, such as semi-supervised learning, open-set recognition, long-tailed learning, grounded generation, and medical AI.

I have been fortunate to collaborate with excellent researchers including [Qiyuan Chen](https://qychen2001.github.io/), [Qian Shao](https://abeier87.github.io/), and my colleagues across academia and industry.

# Projects

## Poco

**Poco** is a cloud-native general AI agent system and a more usable alternative to OpenClaw, with a stronger interface, built-in instant messaging support, sandboxed runtime, and channel-based collaboration.

- 1.3k+ GitHub stars
- Gold Award, Global Hackathon Zhejiang University station
- Third place, AIHackathonTour finals

## YumGo!

**YumGo!** is a food map embedded in Douyin, designed to connect short-form content with local-life discovery and help users turn inspiration into nearby dining decisions.

- Silver Award, Douyin AI Transformer Hackathon

## Agentify

**Agentify** is a natural-language-driven LLM workflow generation framework built on Dify and LangGraph. It uses a dual-agent architecture to plan and serialize workflows, then renders them into deployable HTML interfaces.

- 100+ GitHub stars
- Focused on zero-code agent and workflow composition

## WeDoctor Medical LLM

I contributed to a domain-specific medical LLM project and worked on post-training methods including SFR and reinforcement learning for stronger medical reasoning performance.

- The project previously ranked first on MedBench
